The COVID-19 pandemic, caused by a new SARS-CoV virus (the first version of which caused the 2002–2004 outbreak), seemed to make clear for everyone the importance of global cooperation, solidarity and unity in our societies. It’s the common sense that suggests that, during a state of emergency, humanity, at least for a while, has to combine its resources in order to save lives and save its dignity. Wouldn’t it be total madness if the governments would refuse to stop austerity policies when the health systems are overloaded; if the richest of the rich would keep pursuing immense profits while the rest of the world is experiencing an unprecedented crisis; if the global community would fail to make any collective decision during a global emergency? Wouldn’t it bring our more or less civilized world into a state of some kind of barbarism? However, this is what happened. 


In this designer-as-author project I will try to outline some contours of this new predicament, which is, apparently, at its dawn.
